## Issuer Hub

[Issuer Hub](/issuer-hub/overview) is the workspace where token issuers run their compliance program end-to-end. It houses every product an issuer needs to verify their identity, file regulator-ready disclosures, demonstrate collateralization, and check their assets against jurisdictional requirements — all linked to a single verified onchain identity.

### KYI (Know Your Issuer)

[KYI](/kyi-know-your-issuer/what-is-kyi) verifies that a token is tied to a real legal entity and a verified controlling wallet. It helps platforms answer two critical questions: who stands behind this asset, and does that entity control the wallet with mint or admin authority?

The KYI workflow includes business verification (KYB), wallet signature verification, and an onchain attestation linking entity, wallet, and token. Partners can integrate KYI through the [KYI API](/kyi-know-your-issuer/kyi-api) or embed the flow with the [KYI Widget SDK](/kyi-know-your-issuer/kyi-widget-sdk).

KYI reduces impersonation risk, supports exchange due diligence, and gives wallets, explorers, and compliance teams a reliable token provenance signal.

### Smart Disclosures

[Smart Disclosures](/smart-disclosures/what-is-smart-disclosures) is Bluprynt's AI-assisted disclosure engine for tokenized assets. Teams can upload existing documents, auto-fill structured disclosure forms, and generate regulator-ready outputs in multiple formats.

Key capabilities:

- AI extraction from white papers, terms, and supporting files.
- Guided composition with jurisdiction-specific requirements.
- Real-time feedback on missing or weak disclosure content.
- Final outputs in XBRL, PDF, and DOCX.
- Onchain publication of disclosure attestations.

### MiCA Checker

[MiCA Checker](/mica-checker/mica-checker) is Bluprynt's free diagnostic tool for EU crypto-asset white paper compliance. It lets teams upload a draft white paper and quickly assess alignment with MiCA Articles 4 and 5.

It is designed for:

- Founders preparing a launch.
- Legal teams reviewing disclosure completeness.
- Exchanges pre-screening listing candidates.
- Regulators and analysts running quick document checks.

Teams that need remediation can move from MiCA Checker into Smart Disclosures.

### Proof of Collateral

[Proof of Collateral](/issuer-hub/proof-of-collateral) is the verifiable onchain credential that confirms a tokenized asset is backed by audited reserves of the disclosed type and amount. It is built for stablecoins, tokenized money market funds, tokenized treasuries, and any asset whose value depends on a verifiable reserve.

## Compliance Hub

[Compliance Hub](/compliance-hub/compliance-hub-coming-soon) is Bluprynt's regulator- and compliance-team-facing workspace. It is the operational center for form publishing, submission review, credential issuance, and ongoing supervision.

Core workflows:

- Reviewing disclosure submissions.
- Managing license application pipelines.
- Requesting clarification from applicants.
- Approving or rejecting submissions.
- Directly issuing credentials.
- Monitoring issuer and asset activity.

Together, Smart Disclosures and Compliance Hub create a full issuer-to-regulator operating loop. Issuers submit structured data; supervisors review, decide, and issue verifiable outcomes.

## Credentials

[Credentials](/credentials/credentials-overview) are the verifiable onchain artifacts the rest of the platform produces. They are issued onchain, linked to the issuer's KYI-verified identity, and queryable from any system that needs to validate compliance state.

Bluprynt currently issues two credential types:

| Credential | Purpose |
| --- | --- |
| [Know Your Issuer (KYI)](/credentials/kyi) | Entity, wallet, and token-contract authentication. |
| [Proof of Collateral](/issuer-hub/proof-of-collateral) | Verifiable backing for stablecoins and tokenized assets. |

Credentials are checked by [V4 Hooks](/v4-hooks/overview) before allowing onchain actions, and by the Compliance Hub for ongoing supervision.

***

## V4 Hooks

[V4 Hooks](/v4-hooks/overview) are Bluprynt's onchain integration layer for DeFi. They let any protocol read Bluprynt credentials directly during a pool, swap, or lending operation, so tokenized assets only move through venues that match their compliance posture.

V4 Hooks are how the Issuer Hub and Credentials pillars become enforceable on-chain. Without them, credentials are queryable; with them, credentials are *active*.
