Bluprynt is the **Compliance OS for digital assets**: a machine-readable control layer that links off-chain compliance with on-chain identity, policy, and supervisory visibility.

## Why it exists

Tokenized assets move faster than document-based compliance can handle. Three concrete gaps shape the product:

- **No issuer legibility** — market participants often cannot verify who issued an asset, which entity controls it, or which wallet is authorized to act.
- **No machine-readable layer** — disclosures, approvals, and supervisory actions still live across PDFs, spreadsheets, inboxes, and siloed systems.
- **No machine-speed supervision** — regulators and institutions need real-time visibility into issuer state, not post-facto reconciliation.

## What Bluprynt does

The platform composes four pillars. The home page has the [side-by-side summary](/); the deeper view of each lives on its own page.

### Identity — Know Your Issuer

Every asset issuer on Bluprynt receives a verifiable onchain identity compatible with **W3C DID**, **ONCHAINID (ERC-734/735)**, and **vLEI/LEI**. The identity is cryptographically linked to the legal entity (via KYB), the issuing wallet (via cryptographic challenge), and the token contract (via onchain attestation). This is what [KYI](/kyi-know-your-issuer/what-is-kyi) produces, and it is the foundation every other product builds on.

### Disclosures — Smart Disclosures

[Smart Disclosures](/smart-disclosures/what-is-smart-disclosures) turns regulatory requirements into structured digital workflows: pre-fill jurisdiction-specific forms with AI extraction, complete filings with guided composition, produce structured outputs in XBRL, PDF, and DOCX, and publish each disclosure as an onchain attestation linked to the issuer's identity. The same issuer data is reused across forms and jurisdictions through a shared taxonomy.

### Supervision — Compliance Hub

The [Compliance Hub](/compliance-hub/compliance-hub-coming-soon) gives regulators and supervisory teams an auditable workspace: create and publish forms to issuers, review submissions, issue or revoke credentials, issue credentials directly when no form is needed, and monitor regulated activity. It replaces fragmented review across email and spreadsheets with one operating environment.

### Credentials and onchain enforcement

Attestations record facts. **Credentials** authorize actions. Bluprynt currently issues two:

- **Know Your Issuer (KYI)** — entity authentication.
- **Proof of Collateral** — backing verification.

Credentials become enforceable onchain through [V4 Hooks](/v4-hooks/overview): DeFi protocols read credential state at operation time so tokenized assets only move through venues that match their compliance posture.

## What this is not

Bluprynt is not a blockchain, a custody platform, or a legal-services provider. It does not replace legal systems — it makes them **operable** with on-chain markets at scale.
