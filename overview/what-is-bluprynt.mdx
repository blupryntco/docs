Bluprynt is the **Compliance OS for digital assets**. It gives issuers, regulators, and platforms a machine-readable control layer for identity, disclosures, credentials, and supervision — bound to verifiable onchain state.

## Why it exists

Tokenized assets now move faster than document-based compliance can handle. Three gaps define the market.

  <Card title="No issuer legibility" icon="circle-question">

  </Card>

## What Bluprynt does

Bluprynt turns digital assets into machine-legible, governable market objects. The platform composes four pillars.

### Identity — Know Your Issuer (KYI)

Every asset issuer on Bluprynt receives a verifiable onchain identity that is compatible with **W3C DID**, **ONCHAINID (ERC-734/735)**, and **vLEI/LEI** standards. The identity is cryptographically linked to:

- The legal entity, via KYB verification.
- The issuing wallet, via cryptographic challenge.
- The token contract, via onchain attestation.

This is what [KYI](/kyi-know-your-issuer/what-is-kyi) produces — the foundational identity attestation that everything else builds on.

### Disclosures — Smart Disclosures

[Smart Disclosures](/smart-disclosures/what-is-smart-disclosures) turns regulatory requirements into structured digital workflows instead of static documents.

- Pre-fill jurisdiction-specific disclosure forms using AI extraction.
- Complete filings with AI-assisted guidance and live compliance checks.
- Produce structured outputs in XBRL, PDF, and DOCX.
- Publish disclosures as verifiable onchain attestations linked to the issuer's identity.

The system is built on a taxonomy of standardized concepts, so the same issuer data can be reused across forms, jurisdictions, and workflows.

### Supervision — Compliance Hub

The [Compliance Hub](/compliance-hub/compliance-hub-coming-soon) gives regulators and supervisory teams an auditable operating environment to:

- Create forms and publish them to issuers.
- Review applications and filings.
- Issue or revoke credentials.
- Issue credentials directly when no form is needed.
- Monitor regulated assets and supervisory activity.

This replaces fragmented review across email, spreadsheets, and static documents with one auditable workspace.

### Credentials and onchain enforcement

Attestations record facts. **Credentials** authorize actions. Bluprynt currently issues two verification credentials.

| Credential | Purpose | Key question it answers |
| --- | --- | --- |
| **Know Your Issuer (KYI)** | Entity authentication | "Is this token issued by a verified legal entity?" |
| **Proof of Collateral** | Backing verification | "Is this asset properly collateralized?" |

Credentials become enforceable onchain through [V4 Hooks](/v4-hooks/overview): DeFi protocols read credential state at operation time so tokenized assets only move through venues that match their compliance posture.

## Bluprynt as an operating system

Bluprynt is a digital asset operating system. It does not replace blockchains or legal systems — it makes them operable together at scale.

In practice, that means Bluprynt helps market participants:

- Standardize issuer identity and disclosure data.
- Connect issuer workflows and regulator workflows.
- Turn approvals into verifiable digital credentials.
- Make asset status easier to observe across time and jurisdictions.

## Standards compatibility

Bluprynt's data structures are designed for interoperability with existing identity and attestation standards.

| Standard | How Bluprynt uses it |
| --- | --- |
| **W3C DID** | Decentralized identifiers for asset issuers and assets |
| **W3C Verifiable Credentials** | Schema for attestations and credentials |
| **ONCHAINID (ERC-734/735)** | Onchain identity and claim management |
| **vLEI / LEI (GLEIF)** | Legal entity identification for institutional issuers |
| **EAS (Ethereum Attestation Service)** | Attestation publishing on EVM chains |
| **SAS (Solana Attestation Service)** | Attestation publishing on Solana |
| **IPFS** | Content-addressed storage for disclosure documents |

See [Standards compatibility](/overview/standards-compatibility) for the full matrix.
