Bluprynt is the **Compliance OS for digital assets**. It gives issuers, regulators, and platforms a machine-readable control layer for identity, disclosures, supervision, and credentials — bound to verifiable onchain state.

<Tip>
  In a hurry? Jump to the [Quickstart](/quickstart) and have your first attestation in about 15 minutes. For the deeper read, continue with [What is Bluprynt?](/overview/what-is-bluprynt).
</Tip>

## Why Bluprynt exists

Tokenized assets move faster than document-based compliance can handle. Three gaps define today's market.

<CardGroup cols={3}>
  <Card title="No issuer legibility" icon="circle-question">
    Market participants often cannot verify who issued an asset, which entity controls it, or which wallet is authorized to act.
  </Card>
  <Card title="No machine-readable layer" icon="file-lines">
    Disclosures, approvals, and supervisory actions live across PDFs, spreadsheets, inboxes, and siloed systems.
  </Card>
  <Card title="No machine-speed supervision" icon="gauge-high">
    Regulators and institutions need real-time visibility into issuer state — not post-facto reconciliation.
  </Card>
</CardGroup>

## How the pieces compose

<Steps>
  <Step title="Establish identity">
    A verified onchain identity is created via [KYI](/kyi-know-your-issuer/what-is-kyi) — compatible with W3C DID, ONCHAINID (ERC-734/735), and vLEI/LEI standards. It is cryptographically linked to the legal entity, the issuing wallet, and the token contract.
  </Step>
  <Step title="File structured disclosures">
    [Smart Disclosures](/smart-disclosures/what-is-smart-disclosures) turns regulatory requirements into structured workflows. Outputs are produced in XBRL, PDF, and DOCX, then published as onchain attestations linked to the issuer's identity.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Issue and manage credentials">
    The [Compliance Hub](/compliance-hub/compliance-hub-coming-soon) gives regulators and supervisory teams a workspace to review filings, issue or revoke credentials, and monitor regulated assets.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Enforce onchain">
    [V4 Hooks](/v4-hooks/overview) let any DeFi protocol read Bluprynt credentials at operation time so tokenized assets only move through venues that match their compliance posture.
  </Step>
</Steps>

## Key concepts at a glance

| Term | Meaning |
| --- | --- |
| **Compliance OS** | Bluprynt's control layer unifying identity, disclosures, supervision, and credentials. |
| **Onchain legibility** | The condition where an asset's legal and economic attributes are represented in a standardized, machine-readable, verifiable form. |
| **Attestation** | A verifiable record of a fact about an issuer, asset, or submission. |
| **Credential** | A verifiable permission or status record issued after a review or filing workflow. |
| **CCID (Compliance Chain ID)** | A unique identifier used to track issuers, credentials, and submissions across workflows. |
| **Credit** | Bluprynt's billing unit — one credit covers one published Smart Disclosures document or one generated attestation. |

For the full vocabulary, see the [Key concepts & glossary](/overview/key-concepts-and-glossary).

## Standards Bluprynt interoperates with

| Standard | How Bluprynt uses it |
| --- | --- |
| **W3C DID** | Decentralized identifiers for asset issuers and assets |
| **W3C Verifiable Credentials** | Schema for attestations and credentials |
| **ONCHAINID (ERC-734/735)** | Onchain identity and claim management |
| **vLEI / LEI (GLEIF)** | Legal entity identification for institutional issuers |
| **EAS (Ethereum Attestation Service)** | Attestation publishing on EVM chains |
| **SAS (Solana Attestation Service)** | Attestation publishing on Solana |
| **IPFS** | Content-addressed storage for disclosure documents |

See [Standards compatibility](/overview/standards-compatibility) for the full matrix.
