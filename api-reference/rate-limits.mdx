The Bluprynt Public API applies per-key rate limits to keep latency consistent across all integrators. Limits are tuned for typical exchange-listing, wallet, and dApp workloads.

<Note>
  Specific quotas vary by integration tier and are negotiated when your key is provisioned. Most read-heavy integrations operate well within standard limits; heavier workloads (registry-style sweeps) should request a higher allotment in advance.
</Note>

## Caching guidance

KYI-verified asset records change rarely once issued. Aggressive client-side caching is the easiest way to stay well under any quota:

- Cache `GET /assets/{chain}/{account_address}` responses by `(chain, account_address)` for at least 5 minutes; longer is fine for lower-priority surfaces.
- Cache the listing response (`GET /assets`) per page for at least 1 minute.
- The badge endpoint already returns `Cache-Control: public, max-age=31536000, immutable` — let CDNs and browsers cache forever.

## Backoff on `429` and `5xx`

If you receive a `429 Too Many Requests` (when applicable to your tier) or a `5xx`, retry with exponential backoff:

| Attempt | Delay |
| --- | --- |
| 1 | 1 s |
| 2 | 2 s |
| 3 | 4 s |
| 4 | 8 s |
| 5 | 16 s |

Stop retrying after 5 attempts and surface the failure to the caller. `401` and `404` are deterministic — never retry those.

## Need higher limits?

For real-time market-making, exchange listing pipelines, or large registry mirrors, email [team@bluprynt.com](mailto:team@bluprynt.com) with your expected request volume and we'll provision a tier that fits.
