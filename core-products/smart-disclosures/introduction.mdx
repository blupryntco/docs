**Smart Disclosures** are Bluprynt's structured, on-chain-anchored regulatory filings. They replace the traditional PDF-then-email workflow with a version-controlled, schema-validated, cryptographically verifiable document lifecycle.

## What makes them "smart"

<Columns cols={2}>
  <Card title="Schema-validated" icon="square-check">
    Every field is validated against a JSON schema tied to the jurisdiction, regulation, and asset type. Bluprynt refuses to submit incomplete or inconsistent filings.
  </Card>
  <Card title="Version-controlled" icon="code-branch">
    Each disclosure has a hash, a timestamp, and an author trail. Amendments are first-class: you file a revision, it's linked to the original.
  </Card>
  <Card title="On-chain-anchored" icon="link">
    The final PDF is uploaded to IPFS, and a cryptographic attestation is written on the asset's chain. Anyone can verify the filing without calling Bluprynt.
  </Card>
  <Card title="AI-assisted" icon="sparkles">
    Autofill pulls structured fields from your source documents. Inline AI help grounds every answer in your uploaded materials.
  </Card>
</Columns>

## Who files Smart Disclosures

- **Token issuers** filing white papers, prospectuses, and ongoing disclosures.
- **Compliance officers** at crypto platforms needing to certify asset due diligence.
- **Legal counsel** preparing regulator-ready filings on behalf of clients.
- **Issuers subject to MiCA** — see the dedicated [MiCA Checker](/core-products/mica-checker/introduction) workflow.

## What you get after publishing

- A **signed PDF** — traditional format for counterparties that still need paper.
- An **IPFS CID** — the content-addressed location of the artifact.
- An **on-chain attestation** — cryptographic proof that the disclosure exists and is authentic. Writable to Ethereum (via EAS), Solana (via SAS), Stellar, Aptos, or XRPL.
- A **public-facing KYI record** — the attestation surfaces in the KYI widget and API automatically.

## Why this matters

Regulatory filings have historically been opaque: a PDF emailed to a regulator, cached somewhere internal, pointed to via a weak link. Smart Disclosures make the filing:
- **Tamper-evident** — any modification invalidates the on-chain hash.
- **Globally queryable** — regulators and counterparties read from chains, not portals.
- **Reproducible** — the source form + JSON schema means you can re-derive the PDF.
