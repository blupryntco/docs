**Know Your Issuer (KYI)** is Bluprynt's foundational identity layer. It establishes verifiable links between token issuers and the assets they issue, so anyone — a buyer, a partner, a regulator — can confirm that a given token is legitimately associated with its claimed issuer.

## The problem KYI solves

Today, anyone can deploy a token. There's no way to confirm that the deployer is the entity the token claims to be associated with. Rug pulls, impersonation, and issuer fraud flourish because the on-chain record contains no verified identity.

KYI fixes this by binding three things together, cryptographically and on-chain:

## What a KYI lookup returns

Given a wallet address, a token contract, or a CCID, KYI returns:

- **The verified issuer's name** and their registered jurisdiction
- **Verification status** — KYB verified, license attested, disclosure on file
- **Outstanding attestations** — the full list of active attestations (KYI, MiCA, RegD, proof-of-collateral)
- **Credential history** — licenses, exemptions, and other credentials held by the issuer
- **Links** to the IPFS-stored source documents

## Trust model

KYI lookups are **trust-minimized**:

- All attestations referenced are on-chain, readable with native chain tooling, and cryptographically verifiable without calling Bluprynt.
- Bluprynt acts as the authority that performs KYB and writes the initial issuer attestation — that authority is itself on-chain as a credentialed identity.

Read the threat model in the [Policy Engine white paper](https://github.com/blupryntco/policy-engine).
