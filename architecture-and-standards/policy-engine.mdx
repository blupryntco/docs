The **Bluprynt Policy Engine (BPE)** is the research thesis underlying the platform. It turns jurisdictional regulations, issuer disclosures, and regulator licenses into on-chain enforcement logic — so compliance happens **at transaction time**, not after the fact.

<Info>
  The full technical white paper lives at [`blupryntco/policy-engine`](https://github.com/blupryntco/policy-engine). This page summarizes the key ideas.
</Info>

## The problem

Traditional Web3 tokens enforce no compliance. A token contract can mint, transfer, or burn freely, with no check that:

- The recipient has the required KYC / AML posture
- The issuer still holds the license required to operate
- The jurisdiction of the transfer permits the asset type

Off-chain compliance is brittle — it can be bypassed, it races against the chain, and it relies on every counterparty implementing their own checks.

## The Policy Engine thesis

> Compliance should be executed **before** a token operation, as a precondition for that operation, using on-chain-readable credentials.

## Pre-execution compliance

A policy module is attached to a token contract. When someone calls `transfer`, `mint`, or `burn`:

```
  call transfer(from, to, amount)
            │
            ▼
  policy module reads:
     - from.credentials (KYC, jurisdiction, accreditation)
     - to.credentials
     - asset.attestations (active license, disclosure status)
            │
            ▼
  evaluate against:
     - jurisdiction-specific rules
     - asset-type rules
     - issuer-specific rules
            │
            ▼
  permit or revert
```

The transfer either executes or reverts based on the policy decision. **No compliance is post-hoc.**

## Programmability

Policy modules are code — they can express arbitrary logic:

- **Simple allowlist/denylist** (accredited investors only)
- **Jurisdiction gating** (no transfers to sanctioned countries)
- **Time-based** (lock-up periods per investor class)
- **Reserves-dependent** (ART token: no transfers if reserves below X%)
- **Composite** (all of the above, conjunction / disjunction)

Bluprynt ships a library of pre-built modules for common regulations (MiCA, Reg D, Reg S, MiFID II). Issuers can author custom modules with Bluprynt's DSL.

## Chain coverage

Today, policy modules deploy to:

- **EVM chains** via Solidity — the primary target; works with any ERC-20, ERC-721, or ERC-1155
- **Solana** via program-composition — a reference implementation is in development

The shared principle: the policy module is addressed by the token contract, reads credentials from Bluprynt's on-chain identity registry, and returns a boolean before the operation executes.

## Why this matters

Traditional regulated assets require trusted intermediaries to verify compliance at each step. Programmable compliance lets issuers **embed the regulator's rules directly into the asset** — removing a class of trust assumptions and making the regulated asset equivalent to a bearer instrument *under a rules framework*, rather than under a custodian's supervision.

The consequence is that institutional capital can participate in crypto markets with the same compliance posture they have in traditional markets — but with the transparency and composability of on-chain assets.
