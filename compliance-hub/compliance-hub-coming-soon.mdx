## Compliance Hub

The Compliance Hub is Bluprynt's regulator-facing workspace for supervising digital asset issuers. It gives supervisory teams one operating environment for form management, submission review, credential issuance, and ongoing monitoring.

<Info>
  The Compliance Hub is built for regulatory and supervisory workflows. It replaces fragmented review across email, spreadsheets, and PDFs with a single auditable system.
</Info>

### Overview

The Compliance Hub exists to make digital asset supervision operational. Instead of managing issuer oversight through inboxes, shared drives, and static documents, regulators can run the full workflow in one system. Forms are authored once, published to issuers, reviewed in a structured queue, and converted into verifiable outcomes.

The product is designed around three practical requirements. Supervisors need a clear review process. Issuers need a structured way to file information. Credential outcomes need to be durable, auditable, and easy to verify. The Compliance Hub connects those requirements into one continuous workflow.

Every action is logged. Every submission has a traceable history. Every issued credential can be verified against the underlying supervisory decision.

***

### Who Uses the Compliance Hub

The Compliance Hub is primarily built for **regulators and supervisory teams** that oversee digital asset issuers, service providers, and related market participants. It supports the day-to-day work of publishing forms, reviewing submissions, requesting additional information, approving or rejecting applications, and issuing credentials.

It also supports **authorized institutional participants** that need visibility into issuer status, credential state, and supervisory outcomes. This includes exchanges, underwriters, wallets, and similar organizations that rely on verified compliance records.

**Bluprynt operations teams** use the Hub to support supervisory programs, maintain credential workflows, and handle operational processes where additional review is needed.

### Key Capabilities

#### Form Authoring and Publishing

The Compliance Hub lets supervisors create structured digital forms instead of relying on static templates or one-off document requests. A form can be associated with a specific credential type and published directly into the issuer workflow. That gives regulators a repeatable pipeline from rule intake to review to final outcome.

This matters operationally because the form becomes the starting point for everything else. It defines what the issuer must submit, how the submission should be reviewed, and which credential may be produced after review. The result is a cleaner supervisory process with less ambiguity and less manual coordination.

#### Applications and Filings

The platform supports two distinct supervisory models.

An **application** is a submission that requires explicit review before any credential is issued. This is the right model for licenses, approvals, and other gated regulatory outcomes.

A **filing** is a submission that may issue a credential immediately after submission. This is the right model for recurring disclosures, updates, and other workflows where the submission itself is the event of record.

Supporting both models in one system lets regulators run approval-based and routine reporting workflows without splitting operations across separate tools.

#### Review Workflow

Supervisors review submissions from a structured queue with clear status transitions. A submission can move through states such as **Pending**, **Approved**, **Rejected**, or **Needs More Info**. Each state change is recorded alongside the reviewer, timestamp, supporting material, and decision history.

This gives regulators a more disciplined review process. A reviewer can open the full submission, inspect the attached evidence, request clarification without restarting the workflow, and record a final decision with a durable audit trail. Instead of scattered comments and disconnected files, the full supervisory record stays attached to the submission itself.

#### Credential Management

The Compliance Hub is also the operational center for credential administration. Supervisors can browse credential types, review issued credentials, track status, and manage revocations from a single workspace. This keeps the supervisory workflow connected to the credential lifecycle instead of treating issuance as a separate downstream step.

Credential operations are handled through a managed backend flow. That means supervisors do not need to manage wallets, gas, or blockchain prompts. They initiate a credential action through the interface and receive a simple confirmation flow appropriate for a professional regulatory environment.

#### Direct Issuance

Not every credential should begin with a form. Regulators sometimes need to issue credentials directly for administrative actions, grandfathering exercises, corrections, or other exceptional cases. The Compliance Hub supports this without forcing teams to manufacture artificial submissions just to create a record.

Direct issuance uses the same underlying issuance path as reviewed applications. The difference is only the operational entry point. That keeps the credential system consistent even when the supervisory process differs.

#### Registry and Entity Views

The Compliance Hub provides read-oriented views across organizations, assets, and credentials so supervisors can inspect issuer status outside the submission queue. These views support search, filtering, and detail inspection, making it easier to understand an issuer's broader history rather than only a single filing or application in isolation.

This is important for real supervisory work. Review rarely happens in a vacuum. Supervisors need to see entity history, credential status, prior submissions, and related asset records in the same environment.

#### Supervisory Intelligence

The Compliance Hub is designed for continuous supervision, not just point-in-time review. It can surface market dashboards, asset-level KPIs, active alerts, integration health, and generated reports so regulators can monitor issuer activity between formal submissions.

This changes the role of the system. It is not only a place to process applications. It is also a place to observe regulated activity, identify issues early, and maintain ongoing supervisory awareness.

***

### Operational Model

The Compliance Hub is built around a few practical design rules. Supervisors should not need to understand blockchain operations to use it effectively. Review-based and filing-based workflows should coexist in the same product. Credential outcomes should remain verifiable and programmable even when the interface abstracts the underlying infrastructure. Direct issuance should be available when operational reality demands it.

Taken together, these principles make the product suitable for day-to-day regulatory operations, bulk credential administration, and continuous digital asset supervision.
