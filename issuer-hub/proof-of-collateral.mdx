**Proof of Collateral** is the Issuer Hub credential that gives exchanges, custodians, regulators, and counterparties a verifiable signal that a tokenized asset is properly backed. Unlike a static reserve report, it is an onchain credential bound to the issuer's [KYI](/kyi-know-your-issuer/what-is-kyi) identity, with a defined lifecycle and machine-readable terms.

<Info>
  Proof of Collateral is built for stablecoins, tokenized money market funds, tokenized treasuries, and any asset whose value depends on a verifiable reserve.
</Info>

## What it answers

A Proof of Collateral credential answers three questions about an asset:

- **What backs it?** The disclosed reserve composition — cash, treasuries, money market instruments, or a defined mix.
- **By how much?** The collateralization ratio at the latest verification, with the methodology used to compute it.
- **Verified by whom?** The independent verifier — Bluprynt or an approved third-party auditor — and the date of attestation.

## How issuers obtain it

<Steps>
  <Step title="Complete KYI">
    Proof of Collateral is bound to a verified issuer identity. Finish [KYI](/kyi-know-your-issuer/what-is-kyi) before you start the collateral workflow.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Submit collateral disclosure via Smart Disclosures">
    Provide reserve composition, valuation methodology, custodian information, and the verification frequency you commit to. Outputs are structured for review.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Independent verification">
    Bluprynt — or an approved verifier — reviews the disclosure against the supporting evidence, including auditor reports, custody confirmations, and reserve attestations.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Credential issuance">
    Once approved, a Proof of Collateral credential is published to IPFS, registered onchain via EAS or SAS, and linked to your KYI-verified identity in the Bluprynt Registry.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Renewal">
    The credential carries an expiration aligned with your verification cadence. Bluprynt notifies you in advance; renewal repeats the verification step against fresh evidence.
  </Step>
</Steps>

## What the credential contains

| Field | Description |
| --- | --- |
| **Issuer identity** | The KYI-verified legal entity and controlling wallet. |
| **Asset reference** | The token contract, network, and Bluprynt asset CCID. |
| **Reserve composition** | Structured breakdown of the assets backing the token. |
| **Collateralization ratio** | The reported ratio at the time of verification. |
| **Verifier** | Bluprynt or the approved third-party auditor. |
| **Verified at / Expires at** | Issuance and expiration timestamps. |
| **Evidence references** | IPFS CIDs for the supporting disclosure documents. |
| **Status** | `active` · `expiring` · `expired` · `revoked`. |

## Lifecycle

| Status | Meaning |
| --- | --- |
| `active` | Within its verified period. Queryable onchain and via the API. Counterparties can rely on it. |
| `expiring` | Within the renewal window. Issuer is notified; counterparties can see the upcoming expiration. |
| `expired` | Past the verified period without renewal. Treated as not-current by integrations until renewed. |
| `revoked` | Explicitly invalidated — for example after a failed audit or material change in reserves. Immediate and onchain. |
